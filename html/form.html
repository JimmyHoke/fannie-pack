  • Improve your credit score

      Pay your bills on time. This is the most important factor in your credit score. Even one late payment can have a negative impact.
      Keep your credit utilization low. This means using less than 30% of your available credit.
      Don't open too many new accounts at once. Every time you apply for a new credit account, a hard inquiry is placed on your credit report. Too many hard inquiries can lower your credit score.
      Keep your old accounts open. The longer your credit history, the better.
      Dispute any errors on your credit report. You can get a free copy of your credit report from each of the three major credit bureaus once a year at Annual Credit Report (annualcreditreport.com). Review your reports carefully and dispute any errors you find.

  • Reduce your loan-to-value

    • Make a larger down payment. This is the biggest impact you can have on your LTV ratio. If you can afford to put down a larger down payment, it will significantly reduce your LTV ratio and make you more likely to qualify for a home loan.

  • Reduce your debt-to-income ratio

    • Make a budget and track your spending. This will help you identify where your money is going and where you can make cuts. There are many budgeting apps and tools that can help you get started.
    • Pay more than the minimum payment on your debts. This will help you pay off your debt faster and save money on interest.
    • Consider consolidating your debt. This can help you simplify your payments and get a lower interest rate.
    • Avoid taking on new debt. This may seem obvious, but it's important to resist the temptation to use credit cards to finance new purchases.
    • Increase your income. This can be done by getting a part-time job, starting a side hustle, or asking for a raise at your current job.

  • Reduce your front-end debt-to-income ratio

    • Make a budget and track your spending. This will help you identify where your money is going and where you can make cuts. There are many budgeting apps and tools that can help you get started.
    • Pay more than the minimum payment on your debts. This will help you pay off your debt faster and save money on interest.
    • Consider consolidating your debt. This can help you simplify your payments and get a lower interest rate.
    • Avoid taking on new debt. This may seem obvious, but it's important to resist the temptation to use credit cards to finance new purchases.
    • Increase your income. This can be done by getting a part-time job, starting a side hustle, or asking for a raise at your current job.
    • Get rid of unnecessary debt. If you have credit card debt or other unsecured debt, pay it off as quickly as possible. This will reduce your monthly debt payments and improve your front-end debt-to-income ratio.
